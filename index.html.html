Depreciation

Depreciation is the gradual decrease in the value of a fixed asset over time. This reduction occurs due to usage, wear and tear, passage of time, or technological changes. In accounting, depreciation is treated as an expense and helps businesses determine the true profit by allocating the cost of an asset over its useful life.


Causes of Depreciation

1. Wear and Tear due to Usage or Passage of Time

Assets lose value because of continuous use or simply due to the passage of time. Machines, vehicles, and equipment gradually deteriorate when they are used regularly in business operations.

2. Expiration of Legal Rights

Some assets such as patents, copyrights, and trademarks have a limited legal life. After the expiry of the legal period, these assets lose their value completely.

3. Obsolescence

Obsolescence occurs when an asset becomes outdated due to new technology, improved machines, or changes in demand. Even if the asset is still usable, it may lose value because better alternatives are available.

4. Abnormal Factors

Assets may lose value due to unexpected events such as accidents, natural disasters, fire, or theft. These unusual situations reduce the value of assets suddenly.
